## File types expected

`.docx`, `.pdf`, `.pptx` — both English and Spanish versions

## What Claude will extract

- Community voice (more accessible, less technical than corporate/regulatory)
- Bilingual conventions: when is ES required vs. optional
- Approved Spanish-language terminology (especially technical LNG terms)
- Tone across audiences (concerned resident vs. elected official vs. business partner)
- How to address environmental / safety concerns (honest, specific, respectful)
- Economic impact language (jobs, tax revenue, local spend — how quantified)
- Community commitment language (what NDLNG has promised, how it's being delivered)
- Meeting format (agenda, Q&A handling, follow-up commitments)
- Escalation patterns (who responds to what kind of concern)

## Notes

- Spanish-language materials are critical — capture both "professionally translated" and "community-voice Spanish" examples if they differ. Authentic bilingual voice matters.
- If NDLNG has specific community commitments or mitigation promises in public comms, capture them — Claude should never promise MORE than what's publicly committed, and should consistently reinforce the existing commitments.
- Include any "lessons learned" from past community issues — what tones / phrases backfired, what worked
